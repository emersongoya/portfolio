Conflict and Decision (60 seconds)

The problem, options considered, and strategic choice

‚ö†Ô∏è Conflict

Braskem, through its digital innovation hub Cazoolo, faced challenges in digitalizing sustainable petrochemical products (green packaging). Development teams without established UX standards caused:

  • High rate of rework on sustainable project interfaces
  • Inconsistency in B2B experiences between engineering and operations
  • Risk of delays in launching critical green innovations
  • Absence of common language between designers, devs, and business stakeholders

ü§î Options Considered

Option A: Continuous external consulting for each project

‚ùå Discarded: High recurring cost, low internal autonomy, third-party dependency

Option B: Free tools without central coordination

‚ùå Discarded: Guaranteed inconsistency, impossible to scale, no governance

Option C (Chosen): Trainable and scalable internal Design Thinking framework

‚úÖ Selected: Long-term autonomy, scalable to 40+ professionals, sustainable ROI

‚öñÔ∏è Conscious Trade-off

Sacrificed: Individual technical depth and vertical specialization for each trained professional.

To gain: Massive organizational reach (40 professionals) with horizontal competence in consistent UX and scalable practices.

Reasoning: In the context of heavy industry digital transformation, it's more valuable to have many professionals with basic-intermediate UX competence than a few isolated experts. Shared framework generates critical common language for cross-functional collaboration.

Data, methodology and learnings

üìà Success Proxy

Cazoolo's internal metrics demonstrated a 20% reduction in development cycles after framework implementation. Key indicators:

  • Average UX validation time dropped from 3 weeks to 2.4 weeks
  • Interface rework reduced by 35%
  • 95% of new projects adopted the framework in the first 6 months

üõ°Ô∏è Risk Mitigation

To ensure organic framework adoption:

  • Service Design Mapping: Complete analysis of existing flows before proposing changes
  • Code-Ready Guidelines: Practical templates ready to use in sustainable projects
  • Hands-On Training: Practical workshops with real green packaging cases
  • Champions Network: Identification of early adopters in each technical squad

‚ö†Ô∏è What Got Worse

During framework implementation:

  • Initial overhead: First 2 months had a 15% slowdown while teams learned the methodology
  • Infrastructure gaps: Some necessary tools (Figma, Miro) needed to be acquired, slow bureaucratic process

Learning: Involving senior leadership from the start would have reduced cultural resistance. Initial slowdown was anticipated and communicated.

Estimated impact: 5x greater scalability (from 40 to 200+ professionals) with same time investment, additional 10-15% reduction in dev cycles.
